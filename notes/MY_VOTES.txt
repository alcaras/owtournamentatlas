================================================================
 MY VOTE — Old World S3 map pool (Claude's reasoned take)
 Based on the N=9 atlas sweep + 52 calibration games + the split
 metric. Data: alcaras.github.io/owtournamentatlas/recommended
================================================================

HOW I'M JUDGING (in priority order)
  1. GATE  - capitals land-connected (must) and NOT split (a big
             isolated landmass the capital can't reach is bad even
             if the two capitals connect to each other).
  2. GATE  - sane city sites: 15-25 acceptable, 18-22 ideal.
  3. SOFT  - land-hex distance ~45-65 ideal, 40-70 tolerable.
             Do NOT hard-cut at 65 - some of the most CONSISTENT
             maps sit at 66-69 and that's fine.
  4. PRIZE - CONSISTENCY. It's a blind, no-screen tournament. A map
             with mean 18 / sd 2 is far better than mean 20 / sd 8.
             Low spread is the single most underrated property.
  5. History - Coastal Rain Basin, Continent, Inland Sea are
             battle-tested (52 real games) - bias toward them.

----------------------------------------------------------------
TL;DR POOL I'D RUN (the spine)
----------------------------------------------------------------
  - Coastal Rain Basin — Duel / wide / sym      ★ best all-rounder
  - Continent — Tiny / square (point-sym)       ★ historically core
  - DOTA — Jungle Duel / wide / sym             ★ consistency king
  - Hardwood Forest — Tiny / square / sym       ★ textbook 18-22
  - Arid Plateau — Small Seas Duel / sym        ★ very tight
  - Inland Sea — Duel / (ultra)wide             ✓ historical, solid
  + 1 DOTA variety pick (Sand or Water, Duel/wide/sym)
  + Archipelago — Land Lg·Water Sm Duel/WIDE/sym (the ONE good
    Archipelago - see notes; the ultrawide one is junk)

That's ~7-8 distinct map feels, all ~100% connected, ~0% split,
sites in/near 18-22. Enough variety without the coin-flip maps.

----------------------------------------------------------------
👍 I LIKE  (in-band, connected, low split, low variance)
----------------------------------------------------------------
- Coastal Rain Basin · Duel/wide/sym  — 17.8±2.7 sites, land 55±4,
  100% conn, 0% split. Tight + proven. The flagship.
- DOTA · Jungle · Duel/wide/sym       — 16.4±1.6 (!) sites, land
  56±3. Lowest variance in the entire set. Slightly low sites is
  worth it for how predictable/fair it is.
- DOTA · Sand and DOTA · Water · Duel/wide/sym — same tightness,
  different corridor flavor. Great variety picks.
- Hardwood Forest · Tiny/square/sym   — 21±4 sites, land 56±5,
  100/0. Right in the ideal band.
- Arid Plateau · Small Seas · Duel/sym (ultrawide or wide)
  — 18±1 sites (super tight), land 60-65, 100/0.
- Continent · Tiny/square            — 22±2 sites, land ~48-58,
  100/0. Core historical script; busy but consistent.
- Coastal Rain Basin · Duel/ultrawide/sym — 18.7±1.6, land ~68.
  Land a hair over 65 but extremely tight - I'd still take it.
- Archipelago · Land Lg·Water Sm · Duel/WIDE/sym — surprised me:
  20±3 sites, land 54, 100% conn, 0% split, 99% reachable. The
  data says this one specific Archipelago config is clean. (Only
  this one - see PLS NO.)

🤔 MAYBE  (good, with one caveat — fine if the band is loosened)

- DOTA · Jungle · Duel/ultrawide/sym — land 68 (over 65) but

  arguably the best map in the set. I lean LIKE; "maybe" only
  because of the strict 65 cap.
- Inland Sea · Duel/ultrawide      — 19±1 sites, land ~69±8.
  Historical, solid, but land runs high/variable. Keep for
  heritage + the variety.
- DOTA · Mountain · Tiny/sym       — 22±2 sites, land ~68. Good
  counts, land slightly high.
- Mountain Pass · Tiny/square      — 22±3 sites, land ~50-60,
  100/0. Decent; was worried it ran hot, data says it's OK.
- Desert · None·Std · Tiny    — ~16 sites (low end), land
  ~51. Only if you want a deliberately sparse desert option.

----------------------------------------------------------------
👎 PLS NO  (split-prone, disconnect-prone, or overshoot)
----------------------------------------------------------------
- ALL Donut (Irreg Low, every size/aspect) — chronic: 44-78%
  connected, 44-56% SPLIT. The sea-ring/hole regularly walls a
  big chunk of land away from the capital even when the two
  capitals connect. This is exactly the failure mode we built
  the split metric to catch. Cut Donut entirely.
- Desert · Lush·Std (Duel)    — 67% conn, 33% split, ~13
  sites. Too sparse AND splits. No.
- Wetlands · Duel/ultrawide/sym    — 56% conn, 44% split, land
  77. No. (Other Wetlands aspects also weak.)
- Archipelago · Land Lg·Water Sm · ULTRAWIDE — 56% conn, 44%
  split. Only the WIDE Duel/sym Archipelago survives; drop the
  rest of Archipelago.
- Arid Plateau · Large Seas · Tiny/ultrawide — 27 sites, land
  91, 33% split. Massive overshoot.
- Desert · Lush·Dbl (if it reappears) — 26-50 sites; junk.

----------------------------------------------------------------
PRINCIPLES I'd put to the co-organizers
----------------------------------------------------------------
1. State the 18-22 / 45-65 as TARGETS we tuned settings toward,
   not pass/fail. Of 52 real games only ~35% hit all three; you
   would have rerolled a third of your own history. Don't gate.
2. Objective reroll triggers ONLY (either player, no agreement):
   no land connection; SPLIT (significant land unreachable from a
   capital); absurd sites (<12 or >30). Everything else: play it.
3. DOTA is the fairness/consistency anchor — its sd is ~half
   everything else. Include >=1 DOTA terrain. Jungle = default.
4. Point-symmetry is the tournament default and the fair choice;
   where a sym and nosym variant are close, take sym. (A few of
   my top rows show as nosym only due to N=9 sample noise.)
5. Drop Donut. It "passes" land-connected but the split metric
   exposes it as frequently bisected — bad blind-tournament map.

----------------------------------------------------------------
CAVEATS
----------------------------------------------------------------
- N=9 per config. Means are trustworthy; tails (min/max) noisier.
  Treat sd as the key tie-breaker, not a single bad seed.
- Continent currently Tiny in the data; the Duel-Continent
  variants are sweeping now (you asked) — Duel runs ~14-16 sites
  (leaner, faster) vs Tiny ~22-24. Pick by desired pace.
- "Reachable %" / SPLIT is from one capital; on mirror maps it's
  symmetric, so it's a true map property, independent of the
  capital-to-capital connection (which is the separate gate).
